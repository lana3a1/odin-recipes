Strawberry Lemonade Crinkle Cookies

Sweet strawberry meets zesty lemon in these soft pink crinkle cookies, finished with a light dusting of powdered sugar.

Each bite offers a balanced combination of fruity strawberry sweetness and subtle citrus freshness, with a soft, chewy texture that makes these cookies both refreshing and satisfying.

Ingredients

Cookie Dough

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon strawberry extract
  • Pink food coloring optional, for a soft strawberry- lemonade hue

For Rolling

  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  3. Cream butter and sugar in a large bowl until light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Beat in the egg, then mix in lemon juice, lemon zest, vanilla extract, and strawberry extract.
  5. Add dry ingredients to the wet mixture and mix just until combined.
  6. Tint dough lightly with pink food coloring if desired. Chill dough for 30-45 minutes for best crinkles.
  7. Scoop dough into 1 1/2-tablespoon balls. Roll each ball in granulated sugar, then generously in powdered sugar.
  8. Place cookies 2 inches apart on prepared baking sheets.
  9. Bake for 11-13 minutes, until cookies are puffed, cracked, and set around the edges but soft in the center.
  10. Cool on the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Notes

  • Chilling the dough is essentialfor bold crinkles and thicker cookies; don't skip the 30-45 minute chill.
  • Roll dough first in granulated sugar, the powdered sugarto keep the powdered sugar from dissolving while baking.
  • For anatural strawberry flavor, replace strawberry extract with 1 1/2 tablespoons freeze-dried strawberry powder.
  • Fresh lemon juice gives the brightest flavor, butbottled lemon juice works in a pinch.
  • For softer cookies, remove them when the centers look slightly underbaked-they will set as they cool.
  • Dough can bemade ahead and refrigerated up to 24 hoursbefore baking.
  • Store baked cookies in an airtight container at room temperature forup to 4 days,or freeze forup to 2 month.
